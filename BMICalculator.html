What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple, widely used tool that estimates body fat based on a person’s weight relative to their height. It helps classify individuals as underweight, healthy weight, overweight, or obese.

Healthcare professionals often use BMI as an initial screening method for weight-related conditions such as cardiovascular disease, type 2 diabetes, and other metabolic disorders.

Despite its usefulness, BMI is not a comprehensive measure of health. It does not directly assess body fat and overlooks factors like muscle mass, bone density, sex, age, and overall body composition.
