What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measure used to assess a person's body fat based on their weight and height. It's commonly used to determine if a person’s body weight is within a healthy range for their height. However, while BMI provides a useful screening tool, it is important to note that it does not directly measure body fat. Other factors, such as muscle mass, fat distribution, age, and sex, can all influence BMI. Therefore, for a more complete evaluation of an individual’s health, it is crucial to consider additional assessments alongside BMI.

BMI Categories

BMI is categorized into various ranges to help determine whether an individual is
underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese.
These categories are as follows:

  • Underweight: BMI < 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI 18.5 - 24.9
  • Overweight: BMI 25 - 29.9
  • Obesity: BMI ≥ 30

How to Calculate BMI

  1. Multiply your height by itself (height X height).
  2. Divide your weight by the result from the first step.
International System of Units (SI)

BMI = weight (kg) ÷ (height (m))²

Imperial System

BMI = weight (lb) ÷ (height (inches))² × 703
