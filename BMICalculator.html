What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a widely accepted tool used to estimate body fat levels by comparing an individual’s weight to their height. It provides a quick and simple way to assess whether a person is underweight, within a healthy weight range, overweight, or obese. This numerical value is commonly used by healthcare professionals to screen for potential health issues related to body weight, including cardiovascular disease, diabetes, and other metabolic conditions.

While BMI is helpful as a general guideline, it is not a perfect measurement of health. It does not directly measure body fat, nor does it consider important factors like muscle mass, bone density, age, sex, or overall body composition. For instance, a highly athletic person may have a high BMI due to increased muscle, not excess fat. Similarly, older adults may have a normal BMI yet carry unhealthy fat levels. Because of these limitations, BMI should be used in combination with other health indicators such as waist circumference, physical activity, nutrition, and medical history for a more complete evaluation of one’s well-being.

BMI Categories

BMI values are divided into standardized categories that help interpret a person’s weight status and guide suitable lifestyle or medical interventions. These categories include:

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5 – May indicate malnutrition, underlying health issues, or the need for dietary adjustments.
  • Normal weight: BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 – Generally considered a healthy weight range with a lower risk of weight-related conditions.
  • Overweight: BMI between 25 and 29.9 – May be associated with an increased risk of heart disease, high blood pressure, and other concerns.
  • Obesity: BMI of 30 or higher – Indicates a higher likelihood of serious health risks and typically requires medical or lifestyle interventions.

How to Calculate BMI

Calculating your BMI is straightforward and can be done using either the metric (SI) system or the imperial system. Here’s how:

  1. Multiply your height by itself (height × height).
  2. Divide your weight by the result from step one.
International System of Units (SI)

BMI = weight (kg) ÷ (height (m))²

Imperial System

BMI = weight (lb) ÷ (height (in))² × 703
