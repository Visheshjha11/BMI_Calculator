What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple, widely used tool that estimates body fat by comparing weight and height. It helps identify whether a person is underweight, at a healthy weight, overweight, or obese.

Healthcare professionals use BMI as a screening method for conditions related to body weight, such as heart disease, diabetes, and other metabolic disorders.

However, BMI is not a perfect measure of health. It doesn’t directly measure body fat and doesn’t take into account muscle mass, bone structure, sex, age, or overall body composition. For example, athletes may have a high BMI due to muscle, not fat, while older adults may have a normal BMI but high body fat. For a more complete health assessment, BMI should be considered alongside other factors like waist circumference, physical activity, and medical history.

BMI Categories

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5 – May suggest malnutrition or other underlying health issues.
  • Normal weight: BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 – Typically considered a healthy range.
  • Overweight: BMI between 25 and 29.9 – May indicate an increased risk for health problems.
  • Obese: BMI 30 or higher – Associated with higher risk of serious health conditions and may require lifestyle or medical intervention.

How to Calculate BMI

You can calculate your BMI manually using the formulas below:

  1. Square your height (multiply your height by itself).
  2. Divide your weight by the squared height.

Metric Formula: BMI = weight (kg) / [height (m)]²

Imperial Formula: BMI = (weight (lb) / [height (in)]²) × 703

International System of Units (SI)

BMI = weight (kg) ÷ (height (m))²

Imperial System

BMI = weight (lb) ÷ (height (in))² × 703
